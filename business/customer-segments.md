## Segments Under Consideration

### 1. Wedding Photographers

**Profile:**
- Professional or semi-professional photographers
- Already embedded in weddings
- Serve multiple clients per year
- Tech-savvy, value professionalism

**Pain Points:**
- Delivering photos to couples is cumbersome (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- No easy way to get guest photos
- Want to look professional & modern
- Waste time on logistics vs. shooting

**Decision Maker:** Photographer (would expense to business)

**Budget:** High - Already charge $1,000-5,000 per wedding

**Willingness to Pay:** High (if it saves time and impresses clients)

**Acquisition:**
- Instagram (DM photographers)
- Wedding vendor directories
- Referrals within wedding community
- Cold email via portfolio sites

**Churn Risk:** Low - They have recurring clients

**Competition:** Pixxo, PicEvent, cloud storage

**Advantage for Galeria:**
- All-in-one (photos + attendance + engagement)
- Modern, simpler UX
- Better pricing

---

### 2. Corporate Event Planners

**Profile:**
- Plan conferences, trade shows, company parties
- Work for agencies or in-house
- Professional appearance critical
- Multiple events per year

**Pain Points:**
- Attendance tracking is manual/spreadsheet hell
- Need professional photo sharing for stakeholders
- Engagement activities (lucky draws) are manual
- Must impress executives/clients

**Decision Maker:** Event planner or manager

**Budget:** Medium-High - Events have large budgets

**Willingness to Pay:** High (enterprise deals possible)

**Acquisition:**
- LinkedIn (cold outreach)
- Event planning communities
- Industry conferences
- Partnerships with venues

**Churn Risk:** Medium (job changes, budget cuts)

**Competition:** Cvent, Eventbrite (partial), custom solutions

**Advantage for Galeria:**
- Niche focus on photos + engagement
- Simpler than enterprise platforms
- Self-serve vs. sales-driven

---

### 3. College/Student Organizations

**Profile:**
- Student government, clubs, Greek life
- Low budget but high event frequency
- Tech-savvy, viral potential
- Annual leadership turnover

**Pain Points:**
- Photo sharing after events is messy
- Attendance tracking for funding requirements
- Want engagement features for fun events
- No budget for expensive tools

**Decision Maker:** Student leader (ages 18-22)

**Budget:** Low ($0-10/month typically)

**Willingness to Pay:** Low-Medium (may get university funding)

**Acquisition:**
- Campus ambassador programs
- Student org Facebook groups
- Reddit (r/college, campus subs)
- Word of mouth (viral)

**Churn Risk:** HIGH (leadership turnover every year)

**Competition:** Facebook Groups, Instagram, free tools

**Advantage for Galeria:**
- Free tier is generous
- Features tailored to student events
- Could get viral on campuses

**Verdict:** Good for volume, bad for revenue. Consider as "loss leader" for brand awareness.

---

### 4. Birthday Party Hosts (B2C)

**Profile:**
- Individuals hosting milestone birthdays
- One-time or infrequent use
- Emotional investment in event
- Non-technical users

**Pain Points:**
- Want all photos in one place
- Fun engagement features (lucky draw)
- Easy to share with guests

**Decision Maker:** Individual consumer

**Budget:** Low ($0-20 for special event)

**Willingness to Pay:** Low (one-time, occasional)

**Acquisition:**
- Facebook/Instagram ads
- Pinterest (party planning)
- Word of mouth

**Churn Risk:** N/A (one-time users)

**Competition:** Facebook Events, Evite, cloud storage

**Advantage for Galeria:**
- Better photo sharing
- Fun engagement features

**Verdict:** Large TAM but low ARPU. Hard to build recurring revenue.

## Recommended Approach

### Phase 1: Pick ONE niche

Don't try to serve all segments equally. Pick one and dominate it.

**My recommendation:** **Wedding Photographers**

**Why:**
- High willingness to pay
- Low churn (recurring clients)
- Clear acquisition channels
- They can advocate for the product to clients
- Network effect (photographers talk to each other)

### Phase 2: Expand within niche

Once you have product-market fit with wedding photographers:

- Wedding venues
- Wedding planners
- DJ/MC services
- Photo booth companies

### Phase 3: Adjacent niches

- Corporate events (similar but different)
- Family reunions
- Graduations

## Customer Interviews: Questions to Ask

When validating a niche, ask potential customers:

1. **Discovery:**
   - "Tell me about how you currently handle event photos."
   - "What's the most frustrating part of photo delivery?"

2. **Pain:**
   - "Walk me through the last time [problem X] happened."
   - "How much time do you spend on [process]?"

3. **Value:**
   - "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"
   - "What would solving this be worth to you?"

4. **Validation:**
   - "If I built this, would you pay $29/month for it?"
   - "What would make you decide NOT to buy?"

5. **Referral:**
   - "Who else do you know who has this problem?"

**Example: Wedding Photographer**

```
Name: Professional Wedding Photographer

Demographics:
- Age: 25-45
- Location: US/Canada/UK/Australia (English-speaking)
- Income: $30k-100k+ from photography
- Tech-savvy: Yes

Psychographics:
- Values: Professionalism, client satisfaction, efficiency
- Fears: Looking unprofessional, wasting time, unhappy clients
- Goals: Deliver amazing experiences, get referrals, spend less time on admin

Behaviors:
- Uses Instagram for portfolio
- Uses Gmail/G-Suite
- Mobile + desktop workflow
- Responds to professional messaging, not hype

Decision Process:
- Sees peer recommendation or social proof
- Tries free tier or demo
- If impressed, upgrades for professional features
- Annual billing preferred (recurring expense)
```
